#Lab 2a
### Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Analysis

## 🔍 Objective
Compare the long‑term costs of different printer options by calculating Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) over a 5‑year period.

## 🛠 Steps Taken
- Selected two printers: a budget inkjet and an office laser
- Defined assumptions:
  - Period: 5 years
  - Pages per week: 750 (≈195,000 total)
  - Power usage: 40 hrs/week
  - Electricity cost: $0.30/kWh
  - Paper cost: $6 per ream (500 sheets)
- Gathered pricing and yield data for ink/toner cartridges
- Built a spreadsheet with columns:
  - Item Description
  - Type (Fixed/Variable)
  - Unit Price
  - Units Consumed
  - Total Cost
- Calculated consumables required and total costs
- Compared final TCO values

## 💡 Reflection
- Based on the TCO, which printer is the most cost-effective over 5 years?  

Epson WF‑2930. Much lower total cost than HP LaserJet. 

- Would the answer change for a home user who prints only 5 pages per week?  

Difference disappears. Both cost about the same, so choice depends on features. 

- What other non-financial factors could influence printer selection?  

Speed, print quality, durability, size, noise, extra functions (duplex, fax, ADF). 

- What cost components are more significant for a large workgroup printer?  

Toner/ink, paper, maintenance/service contracts. 

- At what point (in years/pages) do the two-printer options break even in cost? 

With heavy use, Epson stays cheaper all the way. HP never catches up under these assumptions.
